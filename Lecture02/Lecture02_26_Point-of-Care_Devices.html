1. Lateral Flow Assays (LFAs)

Lateral flow assays are paper-based diagnostic devices that enable rapid detection of target analytes in complex samples. They are among the most widely used point-of-care diagnostic tools globally.

Key Components:

  • Sample pad: Where the liquid sample is applied
  • Conjugate pad: Contains labeled antibodies (often gold nanoparticles)
  • Nitrocellulose membrane: Contains test and control lines
  • Absorbent pad: Draws the sample through the device

Working Principle:

  • Sample flows via capillary action through the device
  • Target molecules bind to labeled antibodies
  • Complexes are captured at the test line
  • Excess labeled antibodies bind at the control line

Common Applications:

  • Pregnancy tests (hCG detection)
  • COVID-19 rapid antigen tests
  • Malaria detection
  • Cardiac biomarkers (troponin)
  • Drug screening

Advantages:

  • Results in 5-30 minutes
  • No specialized equipment required
  • Low cost (~$1-10 per test)
  • Long shelf life at room temperature

2. Microfluidic Platforms (Lab-on-a-Chip)

Microfluidic platforms manipulate small volumes of fluids (microliters to picoliters) in microscale channels. These "lab-on-a-chip" devices integrate multiple laboratory functions on a single chip.

Key Features:

  • Channel dimensions: 10-500 micrometers
  • Sample volume: 1-100 microliters
  • Materials: PDMS, glass, silicon, polymers
  • Fabrication: Soft lithography, 3D printing, laser ablation

Integrated Functions:

  • Sample preparation and purification
  • Mixing and reaction chambers
  • Separation (electrophoresis, chromatography)
  • Detection (optical, electrochemical)
  • Multiplexed analysis

Applications:

  • Clinical diagnostics: Blood analysis, immunoassays
  • Molecular biology: PCR, DNA sequencing
  • Drug discovery: Cell-based assays
  • Environmental: Water quality monitoring

Advantages:

  • Reduced sample and reagent consumption
  • Faster analysis times
  • Portable and automated systems
  • High throughput and multiplexing
  • Cost-effective per test

3. Smartphone-Based Diagnostic Readers

Smartphone readers leverage the computational power, high-resolution cameras, and connectivity of mobile devices to transform them into portable diagnostic instruments.

System Components:

  • Hardware attachment: Optical adapters, lenses, LED illumination
  • Mobile application: Image capture, processing, analysis
  • Cloud connectivity: Data storage, AI processing, telemedicine
  • Test cartridge: Sample holder, assay platform

Detection Mechanisms:

  • Colorimetric: RGB analysis, color intensity quantification
  • Fluorescence: LED excitation, filter-based emission detection
  • Microscopy: High-resolution imaging of cells, pathogens
  • Chemiluminescence: Light emission detection

AI Integration:

  • Machine learning for image classification
  • Automated result interpretation
  • Quality control and error detection
  • Personalized health recommendations

Applications:

  • Infectious diseases: Malaria, tuberculosis detection
  • Chronic disease: Diabetes monitoring, cholesterol
  • Water quality: Contamination detection
  • Telemedicine: Remote diagnosis and consultation

Advantages:

  • Widespread device availability (global smartphone penetration)
  • Built-in GPS for geotagging and disease tracking
  • Real-time data transmission to healthcare providers
  • Cost-effective infrastructure utilization
  • Regular software updates and feature expansion

4. Colorimetric Tests

Colorimetric tests produce visible color changes that can be interpreted by the naked eye or simple optical devices. These tests are ideal for resource-limited settings where sophisticated instruments are unavailable.

Detection Principles:

  • pH indicators: Color change based on acidity/alkalinity
  • Enzymatic reactions: Chromogenic substrates
  • Metal complexation: Colored metal-ligand complexes
  • Nanoparticle aggregation: Gold/silver nanoparticle color shifts
  • Redox reactions: Oxidation-reduction color changes

Test Formats:

  • Dipstick tests: Paper or plastic strips with reagent zones
  • Microwell plates: 96-well format for multiple samples
  • Microfluidic paper devices: Patterned paper channels
  • Test tubes: Simple solution-based assays

Common Applications:

  • Urinalysis: Glucose, protein, pH, ketones, blood
  • Water quality: pH, chlorine, nitrates, heavy metals
  • Food safety: Contaminant detection, freshness indicators
  • Clinical chemistry: Glucose, cholesterol monitoring
  • Agriculture: Soil nutrient testing

Advantages:

  • No instrument required for basic applications
  • Immediate visual results
  • Very low cost ($0.10-$2 per test)
  • Minimal training needed
  • Suitable for field deployment
  • Long shelf life and stability

Quantification Methods:

  • Visual comparison to color charts
  • Smartphone camera with color analysis apps
  • Portable spectrophotometers
  • Scanners with image processing software
