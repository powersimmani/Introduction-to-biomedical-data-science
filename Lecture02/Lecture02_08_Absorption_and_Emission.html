Absorption and Emission
Electronic Transitions
Ground state (S₀) → Excited states (S₁, S₂, ...)
ΔE = E_excited - E_ground = hν
Allowed transitions follow selection rules
Vibrational Modes
Molecular vibrations (stretching, bending)
IR absorption region
Fine structure in spectra
Characteristic frequencies for bonds
Selection Rules
Allowed: Δl = ±1 (dipole transitions)
Spin: ΔS = 0 (singlet-singlet)
Symmetry: Determines intensity
Forbidden: Weak but observable
Stokes Shift
λ_emission > λ_excitation
Energy loss to vibrations
Typically 20-100 nm shift
Enables fluorescence detection
Quantum Yield (Φ)
Φ = photons emitted / photons absorbed
Range: 0-1 (0-100%)
High Φ → bright fluorophores
GFP: Φ ≈ 0.79
