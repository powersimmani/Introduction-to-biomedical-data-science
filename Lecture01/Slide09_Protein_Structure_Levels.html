Protein Structure Levels
Primary Structure
Linear sequence of amino acids
Peptide bonds (C-N) link residues
Determines all higher structures
Sickle cell: single mutation (Glu→Val)
Secondary Structure
α-helix: Right-handed spiral, H-bonds between i and i+4
β-sheet: Extended strands, parallel or antiparallel
Stabilized by backbone H-bonds
Tertiary Structure
3D folding of entire polypeptide
Forces: Hydrophobic effect, H-bonds, ionic, disulfide bridges
Native conformation is thermodynamically stable
Quaternary Structure
Assembly of multiple subunits
Example: Hemoglobin (2α + 2β chains)
Cooperative binding and allostery
