Water - The Solvent of Life
H₂O Structure
Molecular Formula: H₂O
Bond Angle: 104.5°
Polarity: Highly polar molecule
Dipole Moment: 1.85 D
Bent shape creates partial charges (δ+ on H, δ− on O), enabling extensive hydrogen bonding
Interactions
Hydrophobic: Water-fearing molecules (lipids, hydrocarbons)
Hydrophilic: Water-loving molecules (ions, polar compounds)
Solvation Shells: Water molecules surround ions
Controls protein folding, membrane formation, and molecular recognition
Unique Properties of Water
High Specific Heat
Temperature regulation
High Heat of Vaporization
Cooling through evaporation
Cohesion & Adhesion
Capillary action in plants
Less Dense as Solid
Ice floats, protects aquatic life
