Chemical Bonds in Biology
Covalent Bonds
Strong (200-400 kJ/mol)
Electron sharing between atoms. Forms backbone of biological molecules (C-C, C-O, C-N bonds).
Ionic Interactions
Medium (100-200 kJ/mol)
Electrostatic attraction between charged groups. Critical in protein structure and enzyme active sites.
Hydrogen Bonds
Weak (10-40 kJ/mol)
Interaction between polar molecules. Essential for DNA base pairing and protein secondary structure.
Van der Waals Forces
Very Weak (2-10 kJ/mol)
Weak attractions between molecules. Important for protein folding and membrane structure.
