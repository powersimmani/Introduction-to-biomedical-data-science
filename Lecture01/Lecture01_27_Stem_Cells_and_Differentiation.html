Stem Cells and Differentiation
Totipotent
Can form entire organism
Example: Zygote, early embryo
First 3-4 cell divisions
Pluripotent
Can form all tissue types
Example: ESCs (inner cell mass)
Cannot form placenta
Multipotent
Limited tissue types
Example: Hematopoietic stem cells
Tissue-specific stem cells
Unipotent
Single cell type
Example: Hepatocytes
Self-renewal only
Differentiation Signals
• Growth factors (FGF, BMP, Wnt)
• Transcription factor networks (Oct4, Sox2, Nanog)
• Chromatin remodeling
• DNA methylation changes
• Epigenetic reprogramming
iPSC Technology (Yamanaka 2006, Nobel 2012)
Reprogramming Factors: Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc (OSKM)
Process: Somatic cells → Pluripotent state
Applications:
• Disease modeling
• Drug screening
• Regenerative medicine
• Patient-specific therapies
• No ethical concerns (vs ESCs)
Regenerative Medicine: Clinical trials ongoing for spinal cord injury, macular degeneration, heart disease, diabetes
