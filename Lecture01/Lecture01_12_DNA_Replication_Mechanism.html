DNA Replication Mechanism
Semiconservative Replication
Each new DNA molecule contains one original and one newly synthesized strand
DNA Polymerase III
• Main replication enzyme
• Synthesizes 5' → 3' direction
• Requires RNA primer
• Speed: ~1000 nucleotides/sec
Leading Strand
• Continuous synthesis
• Same direction as replication fork
• One RNA primer needed
• Rapid and efficient
Lagging Strand
• Discontinuous synthesis
• Opposite to fork movement
• Creates Okazaki fragments
• Multiple primers required
Proofreading
• 3' → 5' exonuclease activity
• Removes incorrect nucleotides
• Error rate: ~10⁻⁹ per base
• Essential for fidelity
Okazaki Fragments: Short DNA segments (100-200 nucleotides) on lagging strand, later joined by DNA ligase
