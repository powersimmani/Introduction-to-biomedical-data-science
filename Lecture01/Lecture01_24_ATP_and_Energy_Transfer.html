ATP and Energy Transfer
ATP Structure
Adenine base
Purine ring
Ribose sugar
5-carbon sugar
Three phosphate groups
High-energy bonds
ATP Hydrolysis
ATP + H₂O → ADP + Pᵢ
ΔG°' = -7.3 kcal/mol
(under standard conditions)
In cells: ΔG ~ -12 kcal/mol
Energy Coupling
Exergonic ATP hydrolysis
drives endergonic reactions
Example: Muscle contraction,
active transport, biosynthesis
Phosphorylation Potential
[ATP]/([ADP][Pᵢ]) ratio
Cellular energy status indicator
Regulated by metabolism
Other Energy Carriers
NADH/NAD⁺: Electron carrier (E°' = -320 mV)
FADH₂/FAD: Electron carrier (E°' = -220 mV)
GTP: Energy carrier (protein synthesis)
Phosphocreatine: Energy reservoir in muscle
Cellular Energy Budget: Human body produces ~65 kg ATP daily; each ATP molecule recycled ~500-700 times/day
