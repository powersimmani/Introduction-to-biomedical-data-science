Generative Models
Principles & Mechanisms

SMILES RNN

Principle: Sequential character-level generation using recurrent neural networks (LSTM/GRU)

Mechanism:

  • Treats molecules as text strings
  • Learns syntax rules of SMILES notation
  • Generates one character at a time
  • Fast and simple, but may produce invalid structures

Graph VAE/GAN

Principle: Direct graph representation with variational autoencoder or generative adversarial network

Mechanism:

  • Operates on molecular graphs directly
  • Adds nodes and edges iteratively
  • Learns continuous latent space
  • Better chemical validity guarantees

3D Diffusion Models

Principle: Denoising diffusion process in 3D coordinate space

Mechanism:

  • Generates 3D conformers directly
  • Gradually denoises random coordinates
  • Captures geometric constraints
  • Produces physically realistic structures

Conditional Generation

Principle: Property-guided molecule generation with target specifications

Mechanism:

  • Incorporates desired properties as input
  • Uses classifier guidance or conditioning
  • Controls specific molecular attributes
  • Enables targeted drug design

Multi-objective Optimization

Principle: Balancing multiple competing objectives simultaneously

Mechanism:

  • Optimizes activity, safety, and ADME together
  • Uses Pareto optimization or weighted rewards
  • Finds trade-off solutions
  • Reflects real drug development constraints

Reinforcement Learning

Principle: Reward-driven policy optimization for goal-directed generation

Mechanism:

  • Treats generation as sequential decision-making
  • Uses reward functions for desired properties
  • Fine-tunes pre-trained models
  • Explores chemical space efficiently
