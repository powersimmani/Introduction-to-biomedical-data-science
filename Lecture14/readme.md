## üìö Key Topics

### Part 1: Ethics in Biomedical AI
- **Beneficence Principles**: Patient safety, risk-benefit analysis, precautionary principle
- **Privacy Concerns**: Data sensitivity, re-identification risks, genetic privacy
- **Informed Consent**: AI transparency, data usage rights, withdrawal provisions
- **Data Ownership**: Patient rights, institutional claims, benefit sharing
- **Algorithmic Bias**: Sources of bias, fairness metrics, mitigation strategies
- **Health Disparities**: Digital divide, representation gaps, access barriers
- **Transparency**: Model explainability, decision rationale, audit trails

### Part 2: Regulatory Framework
- **FDA Regulations**: Software as Medical Device (SaMD), 510(k), de novo pathway, PMA
- **CE Marking**: MDR requirements, risk classification, clinical evaluation
- **Clinical Validation**: Study design, performance standards, real-world evidence
- **Software as Medical Device**: SaMD framework, risk categorization, cybersecurity
- **Quality Management**: ISO 13485, design controls, risk management, CAPA
- **Post-market Surveillance**: Adverse event reporting, performance monitoring

### Part 3: Implementation
- **Clinical Integration**: Workflow analysis, system interfaces, pilot testing
- **Change Management**: Stakeholder engagement, resistance handling, culture change
- **Training Requirements**: User competencies, certification, ongoing education
- **Cost-benefit Analysis**: ROI calculation, quality improvements, risk reduction
- **Reimbursement**: CPT codes, coverage decisions, value-based contracts
- **Liability Issues**: Malpractice, product liability, insurance, risk allocation
- **Global Perspectives**: Regulatory harmonization, cross-border data flows
- **Future Regulations**: AI-specific legislation, adaptive regulations, sandboxes
- **Best Practices**: Governance structures, ethics committees, audit procedures
- **Case Studies**: Success stories, failure analysis, lessons learned
- **Discussion Scenarios**: Ethical dilemmas, regulatory challenges
